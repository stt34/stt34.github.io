BMW Bluetooth Audio Adapter

Project Concept

My project car (a 2010 BMW 128i) did not come with many features that are standard to new cars. Thus, I was forced to use AUX for audio input. I found this cumbersome as I could not charge my phone and play music from it at the same time. Wireless charging was cumbersome and inefficient with a phone mount. In addition, most bluetooth audio retrofits for old cars used FM radio to transmit audio to the car, which seemed lackluster. Thus, I proposed a Bluetooth audio adapter that uses the car’s low‑amperage USB and outputs sound through aux.


Project Timeline

Initial Concept (June 13): Proposed a charging cable that had a bluetooth module piggybacking on it.

Pivotal Shift (June 20): Switched to a CSR8635 Bluetooth receiver powered by the USB port, opting to use the cigarette lighter for phone charging.

Prototyping & Sourcing (June 27 – July 2): Cut a USB‑A→C cable for power, mounted the MakerHawk CSR8635 board on a breadboard, and placed 10 µF + 0.22 µF capacitors in parallel with the battery +/- pads for hiss reduction. I powered the board using its built in micro-USB connector

Noise Elimination (July 3 – 5): Opted to use the battery power +/- pads with a USB A pigtail, finally eliminating low‑volume hiss.

Packaging & Installation (July 6 – 8): Tucked all components inside the center console, secured the board with double-sided tape for easy removal, resoldered and heat-shrinked connections, and routed USB and 3.5 mm cables through openings in the center console.

Validation (July 9 – 10): Confirmed plug‑and‑play pairing, stable audio under vibration, and proper power consumption under the constraints of the 1 A USB port in the car.


Roadblocks & Successes

Roadblocks

  • Persistent hiss until capacitors were properly put in parallel with the power source by using the battery +/- terminals to power the board.
  • Multiple connector attempts (butt splices, terminal blocks) failed under space and wire length constraints before adopting solder and heat-shrink.
  • Always-on nature of the board caused parasitic drain of the car battery, which was remedied by a dongle that allows ON/OFF toggling of a USB port.

Successes

  • Seamless Bluetooth audio streaming without poor or choppy connection.
  • Simultaneous low power audio playback from a single port.
  • Effective unpleasant noise reduction via proper capacitor placement.

ECE Skills Gained

  • Through-hole Soldering & prototype assembly.
  • Noise filtering: decoupling capacitor strategy.
  • Real-world fabrication and integration: power budgeting, troubleshooting, and vibration-proof mounting.

Final Thoughts

This project combined my passion for automotive tech with hands-on ECE practice. It reinforced my problem-solving through hands-on, custom hardware solutions that required me to troubleshoot without documentation. Next, I plan to add an enclosure that protects the device from water and dust. Completing this adapter has deepened my interest in embedded automotive systems, motivating me to explore over-the-air firmware updates.
